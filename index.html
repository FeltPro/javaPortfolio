About Me

Qualified Teacher and Web Designer - I designed a website from raw HTML back in 2008 and published it online. It was blocky, basic and nobody saw it. However, the love of the process always stuck with me and so I'm back to do it properly this time!

Background

Up until August of 2021, I was a full-time Primary School Teacher and teaching lead, and had been for the past ten years. Passing on skills and knowledge has always been something I have loved doing but I had always planned to take a break in my school career to learn new skills myself - it was always something I wanted to do - and coding seemed the obvious choice to me.

I initially dabbled with Python (solely to make a retro Nokia 3310 version of Snake) but found the versatility and ubitquity of JavaScript drawing me in. I considered coding Bootcamps but discovered FreeCodeCamp which I quickly immersed myself in. After cracking a few basic algorithms and discovering the pure rhapsody of using .map() instead of a for loop and Array.push(), I was hooked and made the decision to teach myself Front-End Development with JavaScript.

Simply put, I love the challenges, task-oriented projects, and discovery which coding provides and I plan to keep learning and designing exciting projects. I am pleased with my progress so far; however, I know I am just at the beginning of this exciting journey. I am now keen to develop my knowledge and move onto collaborations and more professional projects.

Clients

I have had the good fortune to work with 'LB Fitness Goals' and design a website for them. As a personal trainer who was looking to promote their brand it is important that they have a website as well as Twitter and FaceBook for potential clients to visit. Together we discussed a simple layout and content of the website and throughout its development, communication has been an important factor. For this website's creation I used HTML, CSS and 'Vanilla' JavaScript to give the NavBar some functionality on mobile phone formats. I also used eye-catching icons which would link to Twitter and FaceBook. Having a range of Media Queries was important to ensure that the layout and images would retain the same quality on a range of devices. As this project is extremely recent and still being developed, it has yet to be hosted with a website domain name. However, LB Fitness has kindly given me permission to use this prototype site as part of this portfolio.
